What is a brief, often proverbial saying that imparts wisdom or a moral lesson?
Adage

Which literary device involves making a direct comparison between two unlike things without using "like" or "as"?
Metaphor

Which literary technique attributes human qualities and characteristics to non-human objects, animals, or ideas?
Personification

What is the repetition of consonant sounds at the beginning of adjacent or closely connected words in a sentence or phrase?
Alliteration

Which figure of speech makes a comparison between two unlike things using "like" or "as"?
Simile

What is a narrative or story in which characters and events represent abstract ideas or moral qualities?
Allegory

Which literary device involves deliberate exaggeration or overstatement for emphasis or effect?
Hyperbole

Which literary technique uses vivid and descriptive language that appeals to the senses to create mental images for the reader?
Imagery

What is the use of words that imitate or resemble the sound they describe or represent?
Onomatopoeia

Which literary device refers to a brief and indirect reference to a person, place, event, or work of literature that the reader is expected to recognize?
Allusion

What is the use of symbols to represent ideas or qualities beyond their literal meaning in a literary work?
Symbolism

Which literary technique gives hints or clues about future events or outcomes in a story, creating anticipation for the reader?
Foreshadowing

What is the contrast between what is expected to happen and what actually occurs in a story, often with unexpected or humorous results?
Irony

Which literary device involves a statement or situation that seems contradictory or absurd but may have an underlying truth?
Paradox

What is a figure of speech that combines contradictory or opposing words to create a specific effect or meaning?
Oxymoron

Which literary term refers to the substitution of a milder or less direct word or phrase for one that is harsh or unpleasant?
Euphemism
